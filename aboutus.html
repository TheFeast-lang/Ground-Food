SDG #2 : Zero Hunger

Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) #2, Zero Hunger, is a global initiative aimed at ending hunger and malnutrition while promoting sustainable food systems. Its primary objectives are to ensure that all people, particularly the most vulnerable populations such as children, women, and marginalized communities, have access to sufficient, nutritious, and affordable food throughout the year. This goal seeks to improve agricultural productivity and incomes, especially for small-scale farmers, and ensure that food systems are resilient to the challenges posed by climate change, economic shocks, and other crises.

The SDG also focuses on improving nutrition by promoting diverse, healthy diets, addressing the underlying causes of hunger, and reducing food waste. Key actions include enhancing the quality of food production, promoting sustainable farming practices, investing in rural infrastructure, and empowering women and local communities to have a central role in food security. By achieving Zero Hunger, the world will contribute to broader goals of reducing poverty, ensuring social equity, and promoting environmental sustainability.
